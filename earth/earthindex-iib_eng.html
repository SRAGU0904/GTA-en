1970

In 1970, there were a total of 651 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 174 deaths and 212 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was the United States. During the 1970s, terrorist attacks in the United States were mainly carried out by left-wing extremists and Puerto Rican nationalists.

1971

In 1971, there were a total of 471 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 173 deaths and 82 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was the United States. Subsequently, the United States established the Committee for the Prevention of Terrorism in 1972, adopting a tougher policy against terrorism and refusing to make any concessions to terrorists who hijack hostages. As a result, terrorism in the United States gradually improved in the following years.

1972

In 1972, there were a total of 567 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 485 deaths and 409 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was the United Kingdom. The early 1970s was the most intense period of the Provisional Irish Republican Army's (PIRA) activities, as they imported a large number of modern weapons and explosives. Car bombings, as a commonly used tactical and strategic weapon, caused a significant number of casualties in the United Kingdom during the "Troubles" period.

1973

In 1973, there were a total of 473 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 370 deaths and 495 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was the United Kingdom.

1974

In 1974, there were a total of 581 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 539 deaths and 865 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was the United Kingdom.

1975

In 1975, there were a total of 740 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 617 deaths and 617 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was the United Kingdom. Subsequently, due to political initiatives led by Mason and strategic changes by the IRA during the ceasefire, the scale of armed actions by the Irish Republican Army in the UK decreased significantly.

1976

In 1976, there were a total of 923 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 674 deaths and 756 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was the United Kingdom. In that year, the terrorist organization known as the Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam (LTTE) was established in Sri Lanka, with the goal of establishing an independent Tamil state in the eastern and northern regions of Sri Lanka. Suicide bombings were the most commonly used method of attack by the LTTE, resulting in the deaths of many high-ranking officials. Additionally, the LTTE carried out multiple terrorist attacks on buildings, commercial centers, and Buddhist holy sites within Sri Lanka.

1977

In 1977, there were a total of 1319 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 457 deaths and 521 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was Italy. The period from the late 1960s to the late 1980s in Italy, known as the "Years of Lead," was characterized by frequent terrorist incidents by both far-left and far-right groups. In that year, there were 91 active armed organizations in Italy.

1978

In 1978, there were a total of 1526 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 1459 deaths and 1600 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was Italy. The extreme left-wing organization known as the Red Brigades kidnapped and murdered a former prime minister, marking the climax of destabilizing assassinations and kidnappings in Italy.

1979

In 1979, there were a total of 2662 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 2100 deaths and 2506 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was El Salvador. From then on, El Salvador experienced an internal war between the pro-American military government and the leftist National Liberation Front, which was considered part of the Central American crisis and the Cold War.

1980

In 1980, there were a total of 2661 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 4391 deaths and 3637 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was El Salvador. In this year, the Salvadoran military and three security forces killed over 10,000 people, most of whom were farmers, union members, teachers, students, journalists, human rights activists, priests, and other activists in popular movements. As the war progressed, the number of murders of church personnel also increased.

1981

In 1981, there were a total of 2586 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 4851 deaths and 3337 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was El Salvador. On March 15, 1981, the Salvadoran military conducted multiple sweeps, resulting in the massacre of hundreds of civilians.

1982

In 1982, there were a total of 2543 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 5135 deaths and 3342 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was El Salvador. From February to April of that year, there were 439 reported acts of sabotage in El Salvador, and from January to September, there were 782 terrorist activities involving explosives or arson. The National Liberation Front also launched large-scale attacks in the capital and temporarily occupied city centers in the country's interior.

The civil war in El Salvador continued. Due to the establishment of free-fire zones, there were large-scale, indiscriminate massacres of civilians.

1988

In 1988, there were a total of 3720 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 7206 deaths and 6960 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was Colombia. In that year, Osama bin Laden established the terrorist organization "Al-Qaeda," with the aim of eliminating Western countries that invaded the Islamic world and establishing a pure and unified Islamic state. Over the years, Al-Qaeda planned multiple terrorist attacks against the United States, disrupting world peace.

1993

In 1993, it was a relatively peaceful year. It was the only year between 1970 and 2021 where no terrorist attacks occurred worldwide.

1994

In 1994, there were a total of 3456 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 7690 deaths and 7569 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was Turkey. In that year, Mohammed Omar established the terrorist organization "Taliban" in Pakistan, with the aim of establishing an Islamic state and implementing Islamic law. The Taliban banned many activities that were previously allowed under the old regime, such as women's employment and education, movies, television, music, dance, and centralized power by eliminating traditional tribal leaders. The Taliban launched multiple terrorist attacks in the Afghanistan region and eventually seized power in Afghanistan twice, implementing extremely radical governance policies that caused great suffering to the people in the region.

1995

In 1995, there were a total of 3081 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 6103 deaths and 14295 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was Pakistan. In that year, Japan experienced the second deadliest terrorist attack in terms of global casualties since 1970. The Japanese terrorist organization "Aum Shinrikyo" released the nerve gas sarin in Tokyo subway cars during rush hour, resulting in the death of 13 people and injury of 5500 others.

1996

In 1996, there were a total of 3058 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 6966 deaths and 10760 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was Colombia. In that year, Sri Lanka experienced the fifth deadliest terrorist attack in terms of global casualties since 1970. On January 31, 1996, the terrorist organization "Tamil Tigers" detonated a car bomb at the Central Bank of Sri Lanka in Colombo, resulting in 90 deaths and 1272 injuries.

1997

In 1997, there were a total of 3198 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 10906 deaths and 8968 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was Colombia. With income from activities such as kidnapping, extortion, and connections with drug traffickers, Colombia's two major terrorist organizations, the Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia (FARC) and the National Liberation Army (ELN), launched multiple armed attacks and bombings against civilians and security forces.

1998

In 1998, there were a total of 934 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 4688 deaths and 8183 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was Algeria. In that year, Kenya experienced the third deadliest terrorist attack in terms of global casualties since 1970. The deadliest attack was the bombing of the US embassy in Nairobi on August 7, 1998. A suicide bomber detonated a car bomb at the US embassy in Nairobi, resulting in 224 deaths and 4000 injuries. This embassy bombing was a retaliatory terrorist attack and sparked a debate in the United States on whether to respond to terrorism with military or legal action. President Bill Clinton immediately ordered cruise missile strikes against terrorist targets in Sudan and Afghanistan.

1999

In 1999, there were a total of 1396 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 3394 deaths and 5405 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was Colombia.

2000

In 2000, there were a total of 1823 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 4394 deaths and 5797 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was India. In 2000, there were church bombings in southern India, carried out by Islamic extremist groups targeting the Christian community. In the following decades, there were often conflicts between Hindus and Christians in India due to religious conversions.

2001

In 2001, there were a total of 1912 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 7729 deaths and 28187 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was India. In that year, the United States experienced the deadliest terrorist attack in terms of global casualties since 1970. The 9/11 attacks were a series of suicide terrorist attacks that occurred on American soil. On September 11, 2001, 19 al-Qaeda terrorists hijacked four commercial airplanes, with two planes crashing into the Twin Towers of the World Trade Center. The attacks resulted in the deaths of 1385 people and injuries to 10878 others from nearly 90 countries or regions around the world. In response to the attacks, the United States launched the War on Terror in Afghanistan to eliminate hiding al-Qaeda terrorists and enacted the USA PATRIOT Act. Many other countries also strengthened their counter-terrorism legislation and expanded law enforcement powers in response to this event.

2002

In 2002, there were a total of 1330 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 4797 deaths and 7081 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was India. Train bombings and attacks on Hindu temples in India were carried out by terrorists.

2003

In 2003, there were a total of 1280 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 3317 deaths and 7384 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was India. In that year, the Iraq War began. Prior to the Iraq War, terrorism in Iraq was not significant. However, after the US invasion of Iraq, the number of terrorist attacks in Iraq began to rise rapidly. Between 1970 and 2021, Iraq has experienced a total of 28,068 terrorist attacks, resulting in a total of 221,956 deaths and injuries. Almost all of these attacks occurred after the Iraq War.

According to Bayan Jabr, the Iraqi Minister of Interior, approximately 12,000 Iraqis were killed in suicide bombings and other attacks by anti-US militants from January 2005 to June 2006.

2010

In 2010, there were a total of 4827 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 7829 deaths and 15953 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was Iraq. On August 18, 2010, the last batch of US combat troops crossed the border between Iraq and Kuwait, and the United States unilaterally declared the end of the Iraq War.

2011

In 2011, there were a total of 5075 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 8246 deaths and 14662 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was Iraq. In that year, Syria experienced a color revolution and quickly developed into a full-scale civil war under the instigation of Western countries. The long-term chaos in the country led to the rapid growth of terrorism and a severe refugee crisis. Large-scale demonstrations erupted one after another, and former Syrian Armed Forces officers and soldiers formed the Free Syrian Army with the goal of overthrowing the regime and implementing democratic reforms. Thus, the Syrian Civil War began. The Syrian Civil War has lasted for more than a decade and has caused at least 490,000 deaths, making it the deadliest war in terms of casualties since the Second Congo War.

2012

In 2012, there were a total of 8525 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 15498 deaths and 25451 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was Pakistan. As part of the war on terror, the Pakistani military launched a war against extremist organizations in the northwest of the country, leading to multiple attacks by insurgents in Pakistan. At the same time, the United States also carried out several drone strikes in Pakistan specifically targeting members of armed groups in the border areas with Afghanistan.

2013

In 2013, there were a total of 12047 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 22279 deaths and 37694 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was Iraq. According to the United Nations Assistance Mission for Iraq, violent conflicts and terrorist attacks in Iraq in December 2013 resulted in 759 deaths, including 661 civilians and 98 members of security forces, and 1345 injuries. In total, various terrorist attacks and violent conflicts in Iraq in 2013 caused 8,868 deaths, including 7,818 civilians, and more than 18,000 injuries.

2014

In 2014, there were a total of 16960 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 44648 deaths and 41143 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was Iraq. In 2006, some terrorist organizations in the Middle East merged to form a larger terrorist organization called the "Islamic State of Iraq," which developed into the well-known "Islamic State" (IS) in 2014. The Islamic State had tens of thousands of fighters and a large number of military weapons such as firearms, artillery, and tanks, posing a significant threat to the security of countries around the world.

2015

In 2015, there were a total of 15138 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 38993 deaths and 44204 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was Iraq. According to a report by the United Nations Assistance Mission for Iraq, violent conflicts and terrorist attacks in Iraq in 2015 caused at least 7,515 deaths and 14,855 injuries.

2016

In 2016, there were a total of 14051 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 35239 deaths and 40576 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was Iraq. In 2016, Iraq experienced the fourth deadliest terrorist attack in terms of global casualties since 1970. On January 11, terrorists launched 60 rocket attacks on Kirkuk, Iraq, resulting in at least 3 deaths and 1500 injuries.

2017

In 2017, there were a total of 11364 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 26897 deaths and 25594 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was Iraq. In that year, the number of deaths from armed attacks in Iraq decreased by 60% compared to 2016, with a total of 3,378 deaths.

2018

2019

In 2019, there were a total of 8537 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 20412 deaths and 18776 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was Afghanistan. According to a report by the United Nations Assistance Mission in Afghanistan, in 2019, conflicts between parties in Afghanistan caused a total of 3,403 civilian deaths and 6,989 civilian injuries, with most civilian casualties caused by anti-government armed groups.

2020

In 2020, there were a total of 8438 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 22847 deaths and 15466 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was Afghanistan. According to Jane's Defence Weekly, in 2020, Afghanistan had the highest number of terrorist attacks, with 2,373 attacks and a death toll of 6,617.

2021

In 2021, there were a total of 4960 terrorist attacks worldwide, resulting in 11545 deaths and 8333 injuries. The country that suffered the most terrorist attacks was Afghanistan. In that year, a total of 1,426 Afghan people were recorded to have died in terrorist attacks. After the Taliban took control of Afghanistan in August, the number of deaths caused by the organization decreased by 32%. However, the terrorist organization ISIS-K began to increase its terrorist activities in Afghanistan, opposing the Taliban.
